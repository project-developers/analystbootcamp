Q & A

Q. Who is a Business Analyst?

A. This is a person who processes, interprets and documents business processes, products, services and software through analysis of data.


Q. What does a Data Analyst do?

A. Collect, clean, study and interpret data sets in order to answer a question or solve a problem.


Q. Is this for only those who have studied subjects related to IT or Computer?

A. No. Everything, just everything we know now, we learned them. We’ve all got the potential. Our Brain never gets full and you don’t need to delete any file for a lack of storage.


Q. Can I become a data analyst without a degree?

A. It is not always necessary to have a degree to get hired as a data analyst. Data analysts are in demand, and employers want to know that you have the skills to do the job. If you don't have a degree, focus on making your portfolio shine with your best.


Q. Can I become a business analyst with no experience?

A. Include transferable skills. Even if you don't have direct experience in business analysis, you likely have transferable skills that can be applied to the role. Keywords like problem-solving, project management, and communication are all valuable skills for a business analyst.
